# Control & Chaos: Game Expansion Ideas

This document lists simple, low-friction 2-player board and card games that can be integrated into the Arcade Terminal. Each game includes a brief summary, why it fits, and how the Lovense toy synchronization can be mapped to its gameplay mechanics.

---

## 🎲 Board Games

### 1. Reversi (Othello)
* **The Rules:** Players take turns placing discs on an 8x8 grid. If you trap your opponent's discs between two of your own, they flip to your color. Most discs at the end wins.
* **Why it fits:** Extremely visual, grid-based, and easy to build.
* **Toy Integration:**
  * **Capture:** Gentle vibration strength scales with the number of opponent discs flipped in a single turn.
  * **Victory:** High-frequency rumble for the winner; deep, slow rumble for the loser.

### 2. Dots and Boxes
* **The Rules:** Players take turns drawing a single line between grid dots. When a player draws the fourth wall of a 1x1 box, they mark the box and get another turn. Most boxes wins.
* **Why it fits:** Simple mechanics, quick turns, and can be easily styled with neon gridlines.
* **Toy Integration:**
  * **Complete Box:** A quick, satisfying pulse.
  * **Double Box (Combo):** A sudden, heavy double-vibration.

### 3. Mancala
* **The Rules:** A board with two rows of small pockets and two large stores. Players distribute seeds around the board. Landing seeds in your store scores points.
* **Why it fits:** Simple mathematical turn-based loop, classic tabletop game.
* **Toy Integration:**
  * **Scoring:** Toy vibrates matching the number of seeds dropped into your store on that turn.
  * **Go Again (landing in store):** A short trigger pulse.

### 4. Battleship (Arcade Style)
* **The Rules:** Secretly place a fleet of ships on a 10x10 grid. Take turns guessing coordinates to strike opponent ships. Sunk fleets lose.
* **Why it fits:** High-tension, turn-based guessing game.
* **Toy Integration:**
  * **Hit:** Instant high-frequency vibration.
  * **Miss:** Gentle pulse for feedback.
  * **Ship Sunk:** Continuous heavy vibration for 3–4 seconds.

## 🃏 Card Games

### 1. Blackjack Duel
* **The Rules:** Standard Blackjack, but played as a direct 1v1 dealer/player swap or closest to 21 wins.
* **Why it fits:** Classic risk-versus-reward card game with zero complicated card combos.
* **Toy Integration:**
  * **Hit:** Small feedback click/vibe.
  * **Double Down:** Medium rumble.
  * **Bust (Over 21):** Intense rumble for the losing player.
  * **Natural Blackjack (21):** Special victory vibration pattern.

### 2. Speed / Spit
* **The Rules:** Fast-paced real-time card game where players try to get rid of all their cards by placing them on active piles (must be +/- 1 in value).
* **Why it fits:** Action-oriented, intense, and breaks the turn-based routine.
* **Toy Integration:**
  * **Match / Placement:** Micro-pulses on every card successfully placed.
  * **Stalemate (Spit):** Small warning rumble when both players are stuck.
  * **Victory (Out of Cards):** Continuous crescendo vibration.

### 3. War (High Card Draw)
* **The Rules:** Each player draws a card from their deck. Highest card wins the pile. On ties (War), three cards are laid down face-down, and a fourth decides the winner of the massive pile.
* **Why it fits:** Zero thought required, highly suspenseful.
* **Toy Integration:**
  * **Round Win:** Small pulse.
  * **War (Tie):** Rapidly rising pulsing patterns on both players' toys as the tension builds.
  * **War Winner:** Receives a massive, rewarding vibration.

### 4. Memory (Concentration)
* **The Rules:** A grid of cards laid face down. Take turns flipping two cards. If they match, keep them and go again. If not, flip them back.
* **Why it fits:** Simple, clean memory gameplay.
* **Toy Integration:**
  * **Match:** Satisfying vibration pulse.
  * **Mismatch:** Very light buzz.
